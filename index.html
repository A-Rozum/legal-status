Expertise and Insights in Action: Your Legal Compass

Dr. Ales Michalevic: Navigating International Legal Challenges

Read our senior partner's interview with "Advocate Street" magazine, where he shares his unique experience and ideas in the field of protection against international persecution:

"In Warsaw and New York airports, I was subjected to full-on detentions, complete with all the paperwork – all because I was allegedly still in their database. Turkey, Montenegro, and other countries gave me what I'd call "residual headaches" – they'd managed to add me to their national databases, where I was still labelled a criminal. In Spain, the police would routinely show up at my hotel after I'd checked in. Germany was a bit trickier; they'd put me in the federal criminal police database. So, any officer who spotted me wouldn't know if it was an INTERPOL issue or not. They'd simply get an alert that I was a criminal who needed to be detained, leaving the higher-ups to sort out the details later. So, you could say I've had the dubious pleasure of experiencing first-hand how this system works – and what it feels like to be the poor sod detained at an airport or border control. Not an experience I'd recommend."

Red Flags: When to Seek Immediate Legal Counsel

Legal and Investigative Actions:

  • Summons from investigators or court
  • Searches of home or office
  • Asset freezes or seizures
  • News of criminal charges or probe

Pressure and Threats:

  • Direct threats to you or your assets
  • Pressure to return to home country
  • Negative media campaigns
  • Officials' negative statements about you

Travel and Immigration Issues:

  • Visa issues or travel restrictions
  • Residency permit complications
  • Entry refusals to certain countries
  • Passport cancellation or non-renewal

Business and Financial Disruptions:

  • Unexpected business audits or probes
  • License issues or regulatory hurdles
  • Bank account closures or denials
  • Increased transaction scrutiny

Or simply a gut feeling something's off.
This list isn't exhaustive. Even if none of these signs are present, trust your instincts.
If something feels wrong, it's always better to consult a lawyer and ensure your rights and safety are protected.
